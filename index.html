Candlesticks — The Language of the Market
Lesson 1 · Master this and you can read any chart in the world
1
Every candle = one time period. On a 1-minute chart, each candle = 1 minute of trading. On a daily chart, each candle = one full day. The candle tells you everything that happened during that time.
2
The body = open to close. A green (bullish) candle means price closed HIGHER than it opened — buyers won. A red (bearish) candle means price closed LOWER — sellers won.
3
The wicks = the extremes. The thin line above the body = the highest price reached. The thin line below = the lowest. Long wicks mean there was a big fight between buyers and sellers.
4
Patterns predict the future. When certain candle shapes appear in certain places, they reliably predict what happens next. That's why traders study them obsessively.
Real analogy: Think of each candle like a sports score. It tells you who was winning (buyers or sellers), how dominant the winner was (big body), and whether there were any wild moments (long wicks). Read enough of these and you start to see the story the market is telling.
✓ Pro Tip: The most powerful candle signals happen at support and resistance levels — not in the middle of a trend. Location matters as much as the candle shape itself.
Support & Resistance
Lesson 2 · The invisible walls that control every price move
1
Resistance = a price ceiling. It's a price level the market has tried to break above multiple times and failed. Every time it gets there, sellers come in and push it back down.
2
Support = a price floor. It's a level where buyers consistently step in to stop the price from falling further. The market bounces off this level repeatedly.
3
When support breaks, it becomes resistance. And when resistance breaks, it becomes support. This flip is one of the most reliable concepts in all of trading.
4
How to use them: Buy near support (lower risk, stop loss just below). Sell near resistance (target). Don't chase price in the middle — wait for the levels.
Real analogy: Support is like the floor of a room. You bounce off it. Resistance is like the ceiling — you hit your head on it and come back down. But sometimes someone knocks a hole in the ceiling (breakout) and the old ceiling becomes the new floor.
⚠️ Rule: Never buy in the middle of nowhere. Only buy near support. Only target resistance for profit. This single rule eliminates most amateur trading mistakes.
Entry & Exit Strategies
Lesson 3 · Knowing when to get in AND out is the whole game
1
Always have a plan before you enter. Before you click buy, you need to know THREE things: (1) Where am I entering? (2) Where's my stop loss? (3) Where's my profit target? If you don't know all three — don't trade.
2
The Risk/Reward ratio. For every dollar you risk, you should aim to make at least 2 dollars. Risk $50, target $100 minimum. This means even if you're wrong 50% of the time, you still make money.
3
Entry signals to look for: Breakout above resistance with high volume. Bounce off support with a bullish candle. Pullback to a moving average in an uptrend. These are high-probability setups.
4
Exit strategies: (1) Hit your profit target — sell. (2) Hit your stop loss — sell. (3) Pattern changes and tells you the trend is ending — sell. Never hold hoping it comes back.
Real analogy: A good trade is like a business deal. You wouldn't sign a contract without knowing what you get if it works and what you lose if it doesn't. Entry and exit planning is your contract with the market.
✓ The 2:1 Rule: If your stop loss is $30 away from entry, your target must be at least $60 away. Always. Every single trade. This math is why disciplined traders survive.
Risk Management & Position Sizing
Lesson 4 · This is what separates survivors from blown accounts
1
The 1% rule. Never risk more than 1% of your total account on any single trade. With $500, that's $5 maximum risk per trade. This sounds tiny — it's not. It's survival math.
2
Position sizing. Your position size is determined by your stop loss distance. If your stop is $10 away and you can only risk $5, you buy 0.5 shares — not 10 shares. The math tells you how many to buy.
3
Never average down. If you buy a stock and it goes against you — DO NOT buy more to "lower your average." This is how accounts get wiped. Accept the loss and move on.
4
Maximum daily loss limit. Set a number — say 3% of your account — and if you lose that in one day, you stop trading for the day. No exceptions. Come back tomorrow.
Real analogy: Risk management is like car insurance. You don't plan to crash. But when you do, the insurance stops the damage from destroying your whole life. Position sizing IS your insurance in trading.
⚠️ Brutal Truth: 90% of traders who blow up their accounts do so because they risked too much on one trade. One trade. The 1% rule means you need 100 consecutive losses to go broke. That almost never happens if you follow the rules.
Market Psychology
Lesson 5 · Why prices really move — and why your emotions will betray you
1
Markets are driven by fear and greed. When people are greedy, they buy everything and prices go up. When they're scared, they sell everything and prices crash. Understanding this cycle is the edge most traders never get.
2
FOMO — Fear Of Missing Out. You see a stock rocketing up. You jump in late because you don't want to miss it. You buy at the top right as everyone else starts selling. FOMO causes more losses than bad analysis.
3
Revenge trading. You lose a trade. You're angry. You immediately take another trade to "win it back" — usually bigger than your rules allow. This almost always makes it worse. Loss + loss = blown account.
4
The winning mindset. Think in probabilities, not certainties. No trade is guaranteed. Accept losses as the cost of doing business. A 60% win rate with 2:1 risk/reward makes you consistently profitable even though you lose 4 out of 10 trades.
Real analogy: Your emotions in trading are like a backseat driver who always panics. "Sell! It's going down!" "Buy now! It's going up!" The best traders learn to mute that voice and follow their system — not their feelings.
✓ Mental Rule: If you feel excited about a trade, be more cautious. If you feel scared to take a trade but your setup is there — that's often your best trade. The market profits from emotional reactions.
Day Trading Rules
Lesson 6 · The non-negotiable rules professionals live by
1
The PDT Rule. In the US, making 4+ day trades in 5 business days with under $25,000 gets your account flagged as a Pattern Day Trader and restricted. Solution: start in Forex (no PDT rule) or use a cash account.
2
Avoid the first 30 minutes. 9:30–10:00am ET is the most chaotic, unpredictable time of the trading day. Wild swings. Fake breakouts. Most professionals sit on their hands during this window.
3
Trade with the trend. If the overall market is going up, focus on buying setups. If it's going down, focus on short setups. "The trend is your friend" is a cliché because it's true.
4
Keep a trade journal. Write down every trade — entry, exit, why you entered, what you were thinking, what happened. Reviewing this monthly shows you your patterns, your mistakes, and your edge.
5
Start in Forex with under $500. No PDT rule. 24/5 market. Micro lots mean tiny position sizes. You can trade real money with $100 and learn without risking your life savings.
